About Me

Most medical imaging analyses focus solely on the final snapshot, but I believe there’s a richer story to be told. By incorporating previous scans into a dynamic time series, my research builds models that capture the full evolution of a condition for enhanced accuracy.

More technically, I extend current longitudinal models to accommodate heterogeneous, heteroscedastic, and correlated time series—reflecting the messy, real-world nature of medical data—to achieve superior predictive performance compared to conventional machine learning models.

I have engineered end-to-end ML pipelines—improving brain cancer classification by 20% and reducing system resource usage by up to 97%—and designed automated decision support systems for NICU ventilator weaning in collaboration with leading healthcare institutions.

Beyond research, I am passionate about teaching and curriculum development. I’ve taught mathematics from middle school to university levels, integrating computational methods and project-based learning to transform study into exploration.

Outside of academia, I am a dedicated strength athlete competing in amateur strongman competitions. The discipline and iterative improvement required in training are values that drive my work in research and teaching.

I am actively seeking technical opportunities in data science, machine learning, and applied research as well as educational roles in math education and curriculum development. Let’s connect!
