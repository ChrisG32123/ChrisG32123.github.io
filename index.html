About Me

Most medical imaging analyses focus solely on the final snapshot, but I believe there’s a richer story to be told. By incorporating previous scans into a dynamic time series, my research builds models that capture the full evolution of a condition for enhanced accuracy.

More technically, I extend current longitudinal models to accommodate heterogeneous, heteroscedastic, and correlated time series—reflecting the messy, real-world nature of medical data—to achieve superior predictive performance compared to conventional machine learning models.

I have engineered end-to-end ML pipelines—improving brain cancer classification by 20% and reducing system resource usage by up to 97%—and designed automated decision support systems for NICU ventilator weaning in collaboration with leading healthcare institutions.

Beyond research, I am passionate about teaching and curriculum development. I’ve taught mathematics from middle school to university levels, integrating computational methods and project-based learning to transform study into exploration.

Outside of academia, I am a dedicated strength athlete competing in amateur strongman competitions. The discipline and iterative improvement required in training are values that drive my work in research and teaching.

I am actively seeking technical opportunities in data science, machine learning, and applied research as well as educational roles in math education and curriculum development. Let’s connect!

Research Projects & Publications

Automated Radiomics Extractor & Kidney Cancer Classification

(Ongoing) In this project, I am developing an imaging modality–agnostic radiomics extractor that streamlines feature extraction across diverse medical imaging pipelines. The system is integrated into a kidney cancer classification framework to facilitate early tumor detection and improve clinical decision-making.

  • Tech Stack: Python, PyTorch
  • Impact: Enhances early diagnosis by standardizing radiomics data extraction.

Brain Cancer Progression Modeling

(Ongoing) I developed a Transformer-based deep learning model that leverages sequential MRI radiomics data to predict glioma progression. This approach, which considers the entire time series of imaging data rather than a single snapshot, has improved predictive accuracy by 20%.

  • Tech Stack: Python, PyTorch
  • Impact: Enables earlier and more accurate prediction of tumor progression to inform clinical interventions.

NICU Ventilator Decision Support System

This project focuses on designing a decision support system for neonatal intensive care units to optimize ventilator weaning. Developed in collaboration with clinicians, the system analyzes patient data to determine the optimal timing for weaning, potentially reducing ventilation duration and improving outcomes.

  • Impact: Aids clinicians in reducing ventilation time and enhancing neonatal care.

Plasma Simulations & High-Dimensional Forecasting

By applying advanced numerical methods and reduced-order modeling, this project enhances the efficiency of plasma simulations. Incorporating additional coupling physics, the system reduces computational costs while delivering more accurate high-dimensional forecasts.

  • Tech Stack: Python, NumPy, SciPy, PDE Solvers
  • Impact: Improves forecasting capabilities and advances plasma physics research.
